Why Sort?

Why is it important to sort your waste?

  1. Food waste is a global issue, with serious local implications. In the US, about 40% of all food produced goes uneaten; majority of the food loss happens at the consumer level, over 60%.
  2. Locally, there are ways to reduce food waste and its negative environmental, economic, and social implications. One approach is to focus on food waste reduction and minimization.
  3. Another approach is to focus on diverting food waste from landfills, which will help prevent the rate of methane emissions from landfills from rising.
  4. UC Davis aims to reach zero waste by 2020. This means that any and all waste will not be sent to landfills, this has great potential for food waste which can be composted or sent to biodigesters (most food waste collected on campus is sent to the Zamora biodigester). To help reach this goal of zero waste, food waste can be sorted and composted on campus or sent to the biodigesters.
